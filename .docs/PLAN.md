A. Turn it into a flagship case study (non-negotiable)

This case study should be data-driven: How much time has the system saved? Has it improved student-teacher interaction or administrative efficiency?

This is the single most important step.

Not a README.
Not a portfolio blurb.
A serious engineering case study.

Structure it like this:

1. Problem framing

Who the users were

What sucked about the old system

Constraints (budget, non-technical stakeholders, deadlines)

2. Architecture

Why NestJS

Why Prisma

Why Postgres vs alternatives

Auth model

Data modeling choices

What you explicitly didn’t build and why

3. Failure & tradeoffs

What broke in production

Performance bottlenecks

Security considerations

What you underestimated

4. Outcomes

Deployed

Paying customer

Subscription model

Active users (even approximate ranges are fine)

This screams “industry-ready.”

This single document can:

Replace 6 side projects

Anchor interviews

Destroy impostor syndrome

D. Make your GitHub credible, not flashy

You don’t need everything public.

What matters:

Clean commits

Meaningful PRs

Architecture decisions documented

Issues that show thinking

Even a sanitized subset of the codebase + diagrams is enough.

People want to see how you think, not the whole product.
